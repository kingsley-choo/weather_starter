# Weather Starter Visual Themes

This document captures the visual theme directions proposed for Weather Starter. Each theme includes its overall character and the main considerations for implementing it consistently across the dashboard.

## 1. Arctic Glass

**Description:** A crisp, wintry interface built from frosted-glass cards layered over a deep navy gradient.

**Design considerations:**

- Use cool blues and whites throughout the palette.
- Apply backdrop blur, translucent surfaces, and thin white card borders.
- Choose a lightweight sans-serif typeface.
- Keep the layout airy and low density.
- Maintain sufficient contrast where translucent cards overlap the background.

## 2. Desert Heat

**Description:** A warm, sun-scorched theme inspired by desert landscapes and baked earth.

**Design considerations:**

- Build the palette from terracotta, amber, and burnt orange.
- Use warm parchment backgrounds.
- Pair bold serif headers with readable body type.
- Give cards heavy shadows and a strong sense of depth.
- Arrange content in a compact tile grid.

## 3. Neon Forecast

**Description:** A dense cyberpunk dashboard with luminous weather data set against dark charcoal.

**Design considerations:**

- Use electric cyan, magenta, and lime as accent colors.
- Set typography entirely in a monospaced font.
- Add restrained glow effects to card borders and key values.
- Favor a dense, data-heavy layout.
- Avoid excessive glow that reduces readability or visual hierarchy.

## 4. Minimal Ink

**Description:** Newspaper-like simplicity with stark typography and maximum whitespace.

**Design considerations:**

- Use a pure white background, near-black text, and one blue accent.
- Choose Helvetica-style typography.
- Use hairline borders and no gradients.
- Remove unnecessary decoration.
- Preserve generous whitespace and a clear editorial hierarchy.

## 5. Storm System

**Description:** A cinematic, high-contrast theme capturing the drama and energy of a thunderstorm.

**Design considerations:**

- Use a deep purple-to-black gradient.
- Reserve lightning yellow for highlights and urgent data.
- Choose condensed, bold typography.
- Add subtle texture to high-contrast cards.
- Use a wide, cinematic layout.

## 6. Botanical Garden

**Description:** A calm, organic dashboard inspired by foliage, natural materials, and garden spaces.

**Design considerations:**

- Use sage green, moss, and cream tones.
- Add subtle leaf-pattern micro-textures.
- Use rounded cards with soft shadows.
- Choose a friendly humanist sans-serif typeface.
- Keep the layout relaxed at medium density.

## 7. Retro Terminal

**Description:** An old-school computer terminal aesthetic optimized for compact, text-forward weather data.

**Design considerations:**

- Use a black background with phosphor green (`#33ff00`) text.
- Set all content in monospaced type.
- Apply a subtle scanline overlay.
- Use flat rectangular cards with dashed borders.
- Organize information in a dense data-table layout.

## 8. Sunset Gradient

**Description:** A warm, atmospheric composition that moves from twilight violet through rose to golden-hour orange.

**Design considerations:**

- Use multi-stop violet, rose, and orange gradients.
- Set temperatures in a display serif typeface.
- Use translucent glass cards.
- Feature one large hero tile supported by a smaller secondary grid.
- Ensure text remains legible across changing gradient colors.

## 9. Paper Map

**Description:** A tactile, cartographic theme that presents weather information like annotations on an aged map.

**Design considerations:**

- Use an aged parchment background (`#f2e8d5`).
- Choose muted cartographic blues and greens.
- Set body copy in a serif typeface.
- Use hand-drawn-style card borders.
- Add restrained compass-rose accents and maintain medium density.

## 10. Dashboard Pro

**Description:** A professional, Bloomberg-inspired interface built for scanning large amounts of weather data quickly.

**Design considerations:**

- Use corporate navy and slate with one teal accent.
- Use Inter for typography.
- Build sharp-cornered cards with little ornamentation.
- Add color-coded data bars for fast comparison.
- Maximize information density while preserving clear grouping.

## 11. Pastel Candy

**Description:** A soft, cheerful theme that assigns playful pastel colors to different weather conditions.

**Design considerations:**

- Use lavender, mint, peach, and baby blue card fills.
- Map colors consistently to weather conditions.
- Use extra-rounded cards and a rounded sans-serif such as Nunito.
- Provide generous padding.
- Keep the interface low density and approachable.

## 12. Obsidian Dark

**Description:** A restrained dark theme with stone-like surfaces and a single warm alert color.

**Design considerations:**

- Use a near-black background (`#0a0a0a`) with stone-grey cards.
- Set primary content in white.
- Reserve amber for alerts and important states.
- Use tight font tracking.
- Avoid decorative elements and maintain medium density.

## 13. Watercolor

**Description:** An expressive, artistic theme with softly bleeding color washes that respond to weather conditions.

**Design considerations:**

- Shift pastel watercolor washes according to current weather.
- Use thin strokes to simulate brush-painted card borders.
- Choose a script font for location names and a serif for data.
- Favor a loose, spacious layout.
- Keep expressive backgrounds subtle enough for accessible text contrast.

## 14. Coastal Breeze

**Description:** A breezy beach-shack aesthetic combining coastal colors with casual chalkboard details.

**Design considerations:**

- Use seafoam, sandy beige, and ocean blue.
- Choose casual rounded typography.
- Draw card outlines with a chalk-stroke effect.
- Use a wide, low-density layout.
- Keep textures informal but avoid making data labels look imprecise.

## 15. High Alpine

**Description:** A crisp Scandinavian-style interface inspired by glaciers, elevation maps, and mountain air.

**Design considerations:**

- Use a clean white base with glacier-blue accents.
- Add thin elevation-line textures to card backgrounds.
- Choose a geometric sans-serif typeface.
- Favor flat design and a tight grid.
- Apply decoration sparingly to preserve Scandinavian restraint.

## 16. Midnight Oil

**Description:** A luxurious nighttime theme with elegant typography, dramatic spacing, and warm metallic accents.

**Design considerations:**

- Use deep navy (`#0d1b2a`), gold (`#c9a84c`), and soft ivory.
- Set temperatures in an elegant display serif.
- Use large, premium-feeling cards.
- Keep information density low.
- Create drama through generous spacing rather than excessive effects.

## 17. Aurora

**Description:** An immersive full-screen experience inspired by the shifting green and violet light of the aurora borealis.

**Design considerations:**

- Layer semi-transparent cards over an animated green-to-violet gradient.
- Use lightweight sans-serif typography.
- Add glow-effect borders with restraint.
- Make the hero area full bleed and immersive.
- Minimize secondary tiles and respect reduced-motion preferences.

## 18. Vintage Radio

**Description:** A warm, tactile interface modeled on vintage radios and Art Deco instrumentation.

**Design considerations:**

- Use warm beige, brown, and rust tones.
- Frame cards with Art Deco-inspired borders.
- Choose condensed serif headers.
- Use dial-style indicators for wind and humidity.
- Maintain medium density and ensure dials also expose precise values.

## 19. Silicon Valley

**Description:** A polished macOS-inspired design built from familiar system-card patterns and comfortable spacing.

**Design considerations:**

- Use light grey (`#f5f5f7`) with blue interactive accents.
- Choose an SF-style system font stack.
- Use borderless cards with large soft shadows and extra-rounded corners.
- Provide comfortable padding.
- Maintain medium information density and clear platform-like hierarchy.

## 20. Emergency Alert

**Description:** A public-safety-oriented interface designed for immediate recognition and rapid access to critical information.

**Design considerations:**

- Use high-visibility yellow and red on white with bold black text.
- Set headers in uppercase.
- Use flat, high-contrast cards with no decorative elements.
- Maximize information density while prioritizing alerts and actions.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate severity or status.

## Choosing a Direction

Any theme can serve as a complete visual system or as a starting point for a hybrid. When combining themes, retain one theme's core layout and typography, then borrow accents or surface treatments from another so the resulting interface remains cohesive.
